Why We Built Voices of Residents

Voices of Residents highlights local voices on issues they observe in their communities. It’s a platform where people can submit concerns, discuss with neighbors, and explore solutions — helping relieve some worries. Local leaders can also use it to better understand residents’ needs and challenges.

Submit an Issue View Issues Join the Discussion

The Problem We’re Tackling

  • Concerns get scattered across private chats and social media, so they’re hard to track or act on.
  • Residents don’t always know where to take an issue—or whether neighbors see the same thing.
  • Leaders want signal, not noise: clear summaries, locations, and context from the people affected.

What Voices of Residents Does

  • Surface issues with simple submissions (title, description, category, photo).
  • Open discussion so neighbors can add context, patterns, and potential fixes.
  • Summaries for leaders that help prioritize and understand impact.
  • Transparency—everyone sees what the community is noticing.

How It Works

  1. Submit a concern with a clear title, brief details, and category.
  2. Explore & discuss issues in your area; add helpful context.
  3. Share summaries with local groups and decision-makers.
  4. Track progress as conversations move toward solutions.

Who It’s For

  • Residents who notice problems and want to be heard.
  • Community groups coordinating volunteers and resources.
  • Local leaders seeking a clearer picture of needs and priorities.

What It’s Not

Voices of Residents is not an emergency service or a replacement for official reporting systems. For emergencies, call 911. For city services, use your local 311 or the appropriate department portal.

Privacy & Safety

  • Only share information you are comfortable making public.
  • We moderate obvious spam, hateful content, and doxxing.
  • Photos should not expose private information or minors without consent.

Impact We Hope to See

  • Faster visibility for recurring problems.
  • Neighbor-to-neighbor collaboration on practical fixes.
  • Better alignment between resident needs and local actions.

What’s Next (Roadmap)

  • Better search & filters across categories and time.
  • Issue status labels (reported, investigating, resolved).
  • Exportable summaries for neighborhood meetings.
  • Optional notifications for updates in your area.

How You Can Help

  • Submit clear, specific titles and short descriptions.
  • Add constructive comments and share local context.
  • Invite neighbors and community groups to participate.
